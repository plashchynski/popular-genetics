Introduction

This is a magazine about genetics and DNA studies. The site consists of the home page with a list of articles. The articles are intended for beginners and non-professionals interested in this topic. All articles were created by me using the information from free sources, mainly Wikipedia.

State 3 things that have inspired you when creating your website.

I am fond of computational biology and genetics. I believe that in the near future biotechnology will be actively developed and used to prolong life, treat diseases, and solve social problems. So I've created a magazine with articles for beginners and non-professionals interested in this topic.

There are other popular science magazines on different topics that inspired me:

  • Quanta Magazine. My favorite popular science magazine with tons of amazing visuals: https://www.quantamagazine.org/
  • Popular Mechanics. A magazine of popular science and technology: https://www.popularmechanics.com/

State 3 ways in which your site is accessible.

I've adapted best practices listed in the MDN Accessibility tutorials, including:

  • Text alternatives for visual content (alt attributes).
  • Responsiveness. The website has both mobile and desktop versions. All articles can be printed with designated printing styles. I use relative sizes (rem) so font size can easiliy be adjasted for people with visual impairments.
  • Unobstructive JS.The website can be viewed on older devices without JS support. Or in case when JS was disabled due to security reasons.

State 3 things you had to learn/find out by yourself when creating your site, and describe how you did that

  • It was the first time I use figma.com to create wireframes and visual pices like the logo.
  • For the first time I tried the Mobile-first approach. I created a mobile version first, and then adopted it for desktop.
  • It was the first time I have not used any presentation things in HTML, like classes "red" or "blue", or "row" and "column". This is pure semantic markup.

What aspect(s) of your work do you think worked well and why?

I think I did a good job of distinguishing between content and visual presentation. I combined different types of visual content: static images, GIFs, SVGs, and even 3D models.

What aspect(s) of your work could be improved, and how might you do things differently another time?

I would like more extensive use of JavaScript. however, I couldn't think of how I can use it in an interesting and useful way within the scope of this website.

The design of the site is probably too minimalistic.
